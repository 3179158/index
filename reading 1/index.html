1.  What are some challenges you face when giving a critique?

A challenge when giving people critiques is the amount of critique that is appropriate and how critical one can be without being harsh. Generally at OCAD during class critiques, people are not very critical or detailed. It is difficult to know how much to critique and to what extant without upsetting people. Especially considering other classmates are not very critical.



2.  How do you handle a critique that you disagree with?

When I hear a critique I disagree with I first explain my intention. Then I ask for more detail and how I can improve. If they don’t understand my intentions and still find faults with it, I accept it. I generally believe in death of the author. At the end of the day it does not matter what I think or what I was trying to convey. Once a work is put out there it is for others to interpret and judge.



3.  Why do you think it’s important to give and receive critiques?

It is important to give and receive critiques because it is necessary to improve a piece. It is also necessary overall to improve as an artist or designer. If no critiques are given to a person, they would go on to make the same errors and create the same work without any variations.
